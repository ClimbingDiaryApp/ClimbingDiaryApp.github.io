1. Scope

This policy explains what data may be processed by the Climbing Diary Android app and the public support website.

2. App modes

  • Trial Premium: a 7-day trial allows the user to use cloud features, friends, groups, challenges and trainings.
  • Lifetime Local Pro: a one-time purchase enables the local diary, projects, sessions, export and advanced analytics without Firebase sync.
  • Premium: an active subscription allows the app to synchronize data with Firebase so friends, groups, challenges, trainings and multi-device sync can work.

3. Data categories

  • Account data: email address, Firebase user ID, and optionally first name, last name, profile name, phone number and profile photo.
  • Climbing data: routes, boulders, projects, attempts, sessions, grades, ascent styles, dates, notes, place type and optional GPS coordinates.
  • Community data: friends, friend groups, invitations, challenges and planned trainings.
  • Technical data: crash diagnostics through Firebase Crashlytics, such as crash information, app version and device information.

4. Android permissions

  • Location: used only when the user chooses to add current GPS location to an entry.
  • Contacts: used only after the user chooses to search for a friend from phone contacts. The app checks whether the phone number is registered in Firebase.
  • Camera and media: used for a profile photo and local attachments for routes or projects.
  • Notifications: used for invitations, trainings, challenges and deadline reminders.

5. Sharing and visibility

Data is not sold. Data may be processed by technical providers required for the app to work, especially Firebase Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase App Check.

The app lets users control profile visibility: search by email, search by phone, phone visibility for friends and profile photo visibility.

6. Data retention

Local data remains on the device until the user deletes it or uninstalls the app.

Cloud data is available during Trial Premium or active Premium. After the trial ends, the user chooses Lifetime Local Pro or Premium. Local data remains on the phone.

Data synchronized with Firebase may be retained for up to 12 months after Premium expires to allow the user to renew Premium or export data. After that period, cloud data may be deleted unless the law requires selected information to be kept for longer.

7. Subscriptions, purchases and ads

Lifetime Local Pro and Premium will be handled through Google Play Billing. A subscription can be cancelled in the user's Google Play account settings. Cancelling Premium may disable cloud features, but it does not automatically delete local data stored on the phone.

At this stage the app does not plan to use ads. Cloud features, friends, groups, challenges and Firebase sync are available only during Trial Premium or an active Premium subscription.

8. Account and data deletion

An account can be deleted in the app: Profile - Data and privacy - Delete account and cloud data.

If you cannot access the app, send a request to climbingdiaryapp@gmail.com. Include the email address used by your app account.

9. Support website

The app website, tester information and bug reporting are available at https://climbingdiaryapp.github.io/en/.
